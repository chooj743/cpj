Activ.Co | Website, Facebook, IG: North, IG: East
For this module, I've decided to group with Joanne Goh focusing on a local all women's gym studio called Activ.Co. Joanne knows about this studio as she has been attending this gym for almost 2 years now. She felt that the website needed help and since this module is the perfect opportunity she went ahead to share with the boss in which she happily agreed to this project.

We both felt a need to get to know more about the owner and her gym better rather than reading from her website but will wait till after the ICE briefing to know what we are supposed to do. In my opinion, this would help to understand what experience and thoughts from a consumers' perspective.

Case Studies of Existing Competitors

While waiting for her response, we've decided to go ahead and research more on her competitors or others of the similar services. I've realised that the commonality that most gyms have is an overall bold typeface, dark coloured theme that shows strength, impact and empowerment. There are two examples in particular that stood out from the rest.

Uppercut Boxing | Website, Instagram, Facebook
At times when I was scrolling through the website I felt that I had to work for it abit too hard. For e.g. when I'm already at a certain page, my mouse have to reach on the top left *cues animation load, click on a category *cues animation load in which would expand and would have to click again to select specific section. This destination alone took 3 clicks and 2 pauses, it might be a bias opinion but I felt that websites should not have much animations to begin with as it would slow down the viewing experience. In instances like this I felt that it is more efficient with all related sections would merge into a single page. As you can see, each page height is relatively short which is kinda wasted space.

The hidden gem of this website was the copywriting done for each of the staff members nicely injected with humour. It is well personalised


The Brave Shapes Co. | Website, Instagram, Facebook
Brave Shapes has a very clear and straightforward webpage that is easy to navigate around. Personally, we felt that TBS is a great example as they had set a consistent tone of voice as a brand throughout and in their copywriting. They managed to balance off the minimalistic aesthetics with casual photography. The photographic style shows the friendly and fun side of the staff which is something great because gyms can sometimes be quite intimidating. Overall it is simple and well constructed, it is nothing too complex that becomes confusing for the (potential) clients which kinda makes it fast to load too it is a good thing.
