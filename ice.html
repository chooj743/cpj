For this module, I've decided to group with Joanne Goh focusing on a local gym studio called Activ.Co. Moving forward we needed to ask her a set of questions in order to get to know the onwer and the gym better.

Information Gathered From Self Research
Activ.Co is an all women's gym founded by Annisa Rahim. The team believes in helping their clients build up their confidences, health or even preparing to transit into motherhood. As of Sep 2020, they have two gyms islandwide; one each in the North and East.

Case Studies of Exsisting Competitors

While waiting for her response, we've decided to go ahead and research more on her competitors or others of the similar services. I've realised that commonality that most gyms have is an overall bold typeface, dark coloured theme that shows strength, impact and empowerment. There is two that examples in particular that stood out from the rest.

Uppercut Boxing | Website, Instagram, Facebook
At times when I was scrolling through the website I felt that I had to work for it abit too hard. For e.g. when I'm already at a certain page, my mouse have to reach on the top left *cues animation load, click on a category *cues animation load in which would expand and would have to click again to select specific section. This destination alone took 3 clicks and 2 pauses, it might be a bias opinion but I felt that websites should not have much animations to begin with as it would slow down the viewing experience. In instances like this I felt that it is more efficient with all related sections would merge into a single page. As you can see, each page height is relatively short which is kinda wasted space.

The hidden gem of this website was the copywriting done for each of the staff members nicely injected with humour. It is well personalised


The Brave Shapes Co. | Website, Instagram, Facebook
Brave Shapes has a very clear and straightforward webpage that is easy to navigate around. Personally, we felt that TBS is a great example as they had set a consistent tone of voice as a brand throughout and in their copywriting. They managed to balance off the minimalistic aesthetics with a casual photography. The photographic style shows the friendly and fun side of the staffs which is something great because gyms can sometimes be quite intimidating. Overall it is simple and well constructed, it is nothing too complex that becomes confusing for the (potential) clients which kinda makes it fast to load too it is a good thing.
